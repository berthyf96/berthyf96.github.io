I work on computational imaging for science. In particular, I develop methods for carefully bringing in assumptions, such as data-driven priors and physics models, to push the limits of conventional optics.
I'm currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at MIT and IAIFI, workiing with Prof. Bill Freeman. I obtained my PhD from Caltech, where I was advised by Prof. Katie Bouman. Previously, I graduated from Princeton University summa cum laude, with a major in Computer Science and minor in Statistics & Machine Learning.
I am currently on the academic job market for tenure-track faculty positions.
